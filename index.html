Republic Day 2021 : India Celebrates 72nd Republic Day Today at Rajpath in Delhi Amid Covid Pandemic

India celebrates its 72nd Republic Day today with a shortened programme at Rajpath in Delhi amid the coronavirus pandemic.

The programme began at 9 am and will conclude after the traditional fly past 11.25 am. Apart from a reduced list of events, the parade will also have a shorter route, no chief guest, fewer spectators, no children under 15 in the annual parade, and fewer soldiers in the Army and Navy contingents.India's military might, cultural diversity, social and economic progress will be on display.

Apart from tableaus of Armed forces, 17 tableaus from different states and nine tableaus from different ministries will also be on display.

Corona Virus Updates

India's daily coronavirus spike at 8-month low with 9,102 new cases-the Union Health Ministry said Tuesday morning.

India reported 9,102 new cases of the novel coronavirus in the last 24 hours, according to the health update by the Union Health Ministry on Tuesday morning. This is the lowest daily spike the country reported after 8 months. With the fresh Covid-19 cases, the tally has now mounted to 1,06,76,838.

On Monday, Delhi recorded 148 fresh Covid-19 cases - the lowest in 9 months, even as the positivity rate stood at 0.31%, news agency PTI quoted authorities as saying. This is for the fourth time that the number of daily cases stood below the 200-mark in January. Meanwhile, the infection tally in the city rose to 6.34 lakh and the death toll mounted to 10,813 with five new fatalities.

Inside the Serum Institute of India

Founded in 1966 in the western city of Pune, the Serum Institute of India has now become the biggest vaccine manufacturer in the world. It is currently producing millions of doses of the Covishield vaccine - developed by AstraZeneca and Oxford University.From Brazil to South Africa, there is no shortage of customers, with governments clamouring to buy Covishield.

Murray Opts Out Of Australian Open

Andy Murray said, he had decided against competing in the Australian Open after failing to find a "workable quarantine" following his recovery from the coronavirus.

Murray has finished his period of self-isolation and had hoped to compete in the first Grand Slam of 2021 in Melbourne.But the former champion said he could not find a way of getting to Australia and then quarantining ahead of the tournament's scheduled start on February 8.He had to head to Australia by Saturday to ensure he could observe the mandatory two-week hotel quarantine period.

After heading to Australia, 72 players set to compete in the tournament have been largely confined to their rooms for two weeks after passengers on their flights tested positive.Players can only leave their rooms for a few hours a day to practice.
